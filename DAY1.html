MY KEDARNATH BLOG...

HOW I REACHED?

My trip to Kedarnath was an unforgettable adventure that combined spirituality, nature, and physical challenge. I began the journey at Gaurikund, where the trek to the Kedarnath Temple started. The trek, spanning around 16 kilometers, was tough but incredibly rewarding, with scenic views of the surrounding Himalayas, forests, and rugged terrain.

"Where divinity meets the mountains—Kedarnaurney of faith and serenity."

AMAZING INSIDE THE TEMPLE!

The closer I got to the temple, the more I was in awe of the majestic surroundings. The Kedarnath Temple, nestled amidst towering snow-capped peaks, was even more beautiful in person than I imagined. The energy there was so peaceful and spiritual, with chants filling the air, and I felt a deep sense of reverence and connection.

Kedarnath Temple – A Divine Abode of Lord Shiva!

  • One of the 12 Jyotirlingas, over 1,000 years old, built by the Pandavas and revived by Adi Shankaracharya.
  • Located at 3,583 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas, surrounded by snow-capped peaks and the Mandakini River.
  • The temple’s stone architecture has withstood time and calamities, including the 2013 Uttarakhand floods.
  • Houses an irregular Shivling, with daily aartis and chants creating a deeply spiritual atmosphere.
  • Believed to grant moksha (liberation), drawing thousands of devotees who brave the challenging trek with immense faith.
  • A place of divine energy, miracles, and spiritual awakening.
